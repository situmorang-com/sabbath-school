Big Idea

Repentance is more than feeling sorry. It is returning to God when the Holy Spirit shows us the truth about our sin. Forgiveness is not earned by our regret; it is received through Jesus. God forgives the repentant sinner, cleanses the heart, and covers us with Christ's righteousness.

Student Note

Sunday - May 31

The Rush of Life

Key Point

Busy lives can make us lose sight of the most important thing: drawing near to Jesus. Sabbath is not only about stopping work; it is about receiving time with God.

Read Together

Follow Along

The lesson points us from distraction to Christ's robe of righteousness. We cannot clean ourselves spiritually. We need Jesus to take away our filthy garments and cover us with His righteousness.

Ask Together

What are the normal things in student life that can crowd out time with God?

Life Step

Choose one moment this week to pause, pray, and sit with Jesus before rushing into the next task.

Monday - June 1

Holy Spirit Promptings

Key Point

The Holy Spirit convicts us of sin so that our relationship with God can be restored. Conviction is uncomfortable, but it is a gift.

Read Together

Follow Along

It is easy to move from remorse to excuse-making. Real spiritual growth begins when we stop defending our sin and listen to God's voice.

Ask Together

How can you tell the difference between hopeless shame and the Holy Spirit's conviction?

Answer Guide

Shame says, "Hide from God." Conviction says, "Return to God."

Tuesday - June 2

Real Repentance

Key Point

True repentance includes sincere sorrow for sin and a surrendered decision to turn away from it.

Read Together

Follow Along

The world often says, "You are fine as you are." The gospel says, "Come to Jesus as you are, and let Him change you." Repentance is not self-improvement; it is surrender.

Ask Together

What is the difference between being sorry you got caught and being sorry for the sin itself?

Student Note

Wednesday - June 3

Sufficient Grace

Key Point

God's grace is sufficient for the repentant sinner. He is merciful, gracious, patient, and abundant in goodness and truth.

Read Together

Follow Along

After Israel's serious failure, God revealed His character to Moses. Grace does not make sin small; grace shows that God's mercy is greater than our failure when we return to Him.

Ask Together

Why is it important to know God's character when we feel guilty?

Life Step

When guilt comes, do not run away from God. Pray honestly: "Lord, be merciful to me. I need Your grace."

Thursday - June 4

The Most Expensive Robe

Key Point

The wedding garment points to Christ's righteousness. We cannot stand before God in our own goodness.

Read Together

Follow Along

In the parable, the man without the wedding garment had been invited but refused the covering provided. The lesson is clear: salvation is not self-made. Christ gives the robe we could never make.

Ask Together

What does it mean in daily life to accept Christ's robe of righteousness?

Answer Guide

It means trusting Jesus instead of our own goodness, confessing sin instead of hiding it, and allowing His grace to change our conduct.

Friday - June 5

Further Thought

Key Point

Hosea uses farming language: break up the hard ground, seek the Lord, and let Him rain righteousness on you. A hard heart can become ready soil for God's work.

Read Together

Follow Along

Repentance is not a one-time classroom topic. It is part of a living relationship with God. We turn toward Him, cling to Him, and ask Him to keep working in us.

Ask Together

Which part is hardest for you: confessing, receiving forgiveness, making things right, or changing direction?

Weekly Challenge

  1. Pause when the Holy Spirit convicts you.
  2. Confess honestly to God.
  3. Receive forgiveness by faith.
  4. Make things right with someone if needed.
  5. Ask Jesus for power to walk differently.

Closing Appeal

God is not asking you to clean yourself before you come to Him. He is asking you to come to Him so He can forgive and cleanse you. Do not hide from the only One who can heal you.

Prayer thought: Lord, give us honest hearts. Help us listen when Your Spirit speaks. Teach us to confess without fear, receive Your forgiveness by faith, and wear the righteousness of Jesus instead of trusting ourselves. Amen.
